Asteron

2nd planet from Helyra
Classification: terrestrial planet

About the Planet

Asteron is a terrestrial desert planet characterized by its arid landscape, sparse vegetation, and extreme temperature variations between day and night. Its surface is dominated by vast deserts, rocky plateaus, and occasional mountain ranges. The design this world was inspired by the climate of mars and mercury and the harsh and rugged terrains found in Earth's most extreme desert regions, such as the sahara and the grand canyons.

Planet Orbit

Solar Orbit

mildly elliptical orbit but still remains stable. Distance from the sun is approximately 0.7 Astronomical Units (105 million kilometers)

Rotation

A slower rotation, One day on Asteron equals approximately 200hrs on earth.

Planet geology

Geological Features

The Asteron’s surface is iron rich, giving it’s red and ocher tones as seen from orbit. Massive dust devils near the surface of the northern hemisphere. Most of the surface consist of desert bastions and and wind cover canyons. Ancient riverbeds and subsurface aquifers hint that Asteron once had oceans and rivers on it’s surface.

Internal Structure

Crust: The planet’s crust is iron-rich, giving the red and ochre tones seen from orbit. loss sandy upper crust.
Mantle: Solid silicate rock with convection currents driving tectonic activity.
Core: Metallic iron-nickel core generating a weak magnetic field.

Planet atmosphere

Atmospheric Composition

Thin but stable, composed primarily of carbon dioxide and nitrogen with trace argon. Frequent dust storms sweep across its equatorial region.

Climate and Weather

Extreme temperature variations between day and night, with scorching hot days and freezing cold nights. Occasional dust storms and minimal precipitation characterize the planet's harsh climate.

Orbiting Bodies

Moons

One small, potato-shaped moon (Kareth), possibly a captured asteroid, caught in Asteron’s Gravitational pull.
