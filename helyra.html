Brief description of the Star

About the Star

helyra is a red dwarf, a pre main sequence star of spectral type G-type main-sequence star (G dwarf). Helyra is orbited by six unique worlds. helyra's design is inspired by a cobination of yellow main sequence stars such as Earth's sun as well as NASA research on stars and their lifecycles.

Orbiting Bodies

Orbiting Bodies

Helyra is orbited by six diverse planets, each with its own unique characteristics and environments. These planets range from terrestrial worlds to gas giants and ice giants, showcasing a variety of planetary types within the same star system. The planets orbit Helyra at varying distances, resulting in different orbital periods and climatic conditions. Some of these world fall within Helyra's habitiable zone making them suitble for life. This diversity makes the Helyra star system a fascinating subject of study for astronomers and planetary scientists alike.

Stellar Classification

Stellar Classification

Helyra is a T Tauri Star (early stage of a Sun-like stars): For stars like our sun, once the surrounding cloud of gas and dust has mostly dissipated, the star enters a T Tauri phase, also known as the pre-main-sequence (PMS) phase. This phase is characterized by strong stellar winds, variability in brightness, and the presence of a circumstellar disk from which planets may eventually form. Helyra is now entering it's main sequence phase, meaning Pyronis may be the last world that forms within Helyra's system. this also brightens the potential of life forming on on worlds such as Thalassa, Asteron and Gaia
