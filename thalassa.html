Thalassa

Forth planet from the star
classification: water world

About Thalassa

Thalassa is a captivating water world, entirely covered by a vast global ocean with no landmasses. The planet's surface is dominated by deep blue waters, with varying shades indicating different ocean depths. Thalassa's design is inspired by the concept of ocean planets observed in exoplanet research, where water constitutes the majority of the planet's mass.

Planet Orbit

Solar Orbit

Thalassa orbits Helyra at an average distance of 2.5 Astronomical Units (375 million Kilometers), completing one orbit every 800 Earth days. This distance places Thalassa just beyond the habitable zone, resulting in colder temperatures and a frozen surface layer beneath its deep oceans.

Rotation and Tilt

Thalassa has a relatively slow rotation period of 30 hours, leading to longer days and nights compared to Earth. The planet's axial tilt is minimal, resulting in negligible seasonal variations throughout its orbit.

Planet geology

Surface Composition

Thalassa's surface is entirely covered by a deep global ocean, with no landmasses. The ocean is composed primarily of water, with traces of salts and minerals that contribute to its unique chemical makeup.

Structure

crust: a thick ice layer covering the ocean, thickest at the north and south poles
ocean: global ocean with depths reaching several kilometers
mantle: silicate rock layer beneath the ocean
core: iron and nickel

Planet atmosphere

Atmospheric Composition

Thalassa's atmosphere is primarily composed of nitrogen (70%) and oxygen (25%), with trace amounts of water vapor and carbon dioxide. The atmosphere is relatively thin, resulting in lower surface pressure compared to Earth.

Climate and Weather

The climate of Thalassa is characterized by stable temperatures due to the moderating effect of its vast oceans. Weather patterns include gentle winds and occasional storms, with precipitation occurring mainly as light rain or snow fall over the ocean surface.

Orbiting Bodies

Moons

Thalassa is orbited by two small moons, Nereid and Proteus, which are irregularly shaped and likely captured asteroids. These moons contribute to tidal forces affecting Thalassa's ocean dynamics.
